About Me

Who I Am

I am a postdoctoral researcher at Imperial College London working at the intersection of radar signal processing, machine learning, and digital health. My research focuses on the design, development, validation, and optimisation of algorithms for digital biomarker extraction and analysis, enabling continuous, passive monitoring of human movement and physiological signals in real-world environments.

During my PhD within the Next Generation Neural Interfaces (NGNI) Lab at the Department of Electrical and Electronic Engineering, Imperial College London, I developed radar-based methods for human motion analysis, with applications in remote gait monitoring, assisted living, and neurodegenerative disorder assessment. This work explored novel radar-based signal processing techniques to extract clinically relevant gait parameters for continuous, unobtrusive home-based monitoring. A central application was the early detection of health deterioration in older adults — particularly those living with neurological conditions such as Parkinson's Disease — by capturing clinically meaningful gait and behavioural data outside the clinic, continuously and without user burden.

I am also a co-founder of Zoiva, a digital health spinout from Imperial College London translating radar-based health monitoring research into real-world clinical and commercial impact.

Research

Research Interests

📡

Radar Signal Processing

UWB and 60 GHz FMCW radar systems; clutter suppression, STFT spectrograms, micro-Doppler analysis, and keystone processing.

🚶

Gait Analysis

Spatiotemporal feature extraction, gait event detection, and stride parameter estimation from radar Doppler signatures.

🧠

Neurological Health Monitoring

Continuous, passive monitoring of Parkinson's Disease symptoms — gait impairment, motor variability, activity, and fall risk.

👥

Multi-Person Tracking

Kalman filter and JPDA/Hungarian algorithm frameworks for simultaneous multi-target detection and tracking with radar.

🤖

Machine Learning for Health

Classification and anomaly detection pipelines for distinguishing PD from healthy gait; feature engineering from radar data.

❤️

Physiological Signal Estimation

Non-contact heart rate and respiration rate estimation using micro-Doppler radar in free-living environments.

Parkinson's Disease Gait Monitoring

End-to-end pipeline for radar-based PD gait analysis: clutter suppression, STFT spectrogram generation, gait event detection, and spatiotemporal feature extraction. Classification pipeline distinguishing PD from healthy cohorts using data from multi-radar UWB trials.

Research

Multi-Person Radar Tracking

Multi-target tracking system combining Kalman filtering with JPDA and Hungarian algorithm data association. Simultaneously tracks multiple individuals from a single radar, with simulated Boulic-Thalmann synthetic human models for algorithm validation.

Kalman Filter JPDA FMCW MATLAB

Real-time interactive demonstration system using a UWB radar for contactless hand position tracking to control a Pong game. Developed for public engagement and outreach, showcasing real-world radar sensing capabilities in an intuitive, interactive format.
